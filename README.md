## Week 1 – Basics of GAN

In the first week, I concentrated on learning the basics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and how they are used to create realistic synthetic data.

### Topics Covered

* **Generative Models Introduction**

  * Gained knowledge about Generative vs Discriminative models.
  * Focused on how GANs learn data distribution rather than data classification.

* **GAN Architecture**

  * Learned about the general architecture of GANs.
  * Got insight into the working of both Generator and Discriminator.

* **Generator**

  * Receives input in the form of random noise vector (or latent vector).
  * Trains to generate realistic images by learning the training dataset.
  * Keeps improving as it gets feedback from the Discriminator.

* **Discriminator**

  * Functions like a binary classifier.
  * Learns to classify whether the image is genuine from the dataset or it is a fake image generated by the generator.
  * Gives feedback for the generator to get better.

* **Adversarial Learning Process**

  * Learned about the alternated training of both the networks.
  * Focused on the competitive learning process.

* **Minimax Objective Function**

  * Investigated the math objective function that serves the purpose of training GANs.
  * Comprehended how the Generator tries to minimize the loss, while Discriminator maximizes its ability to classify images.

* **Latent Space**

  * Investigated how random noise vectors are chosen from the latent space.
  * Comprehended how these vectors are translated into images through the Generator.

* **Training Challenges**

  * Mode Collapse
  * Vanishing Gradients
  * Training Instability
  * Hyperparameter Sensitivity
  * Comprehended how balance between the Generator and Discriminator is vital for successful training.

* **GAN Variants**

  * Explored the fundamental concept of different GAN architectures such as:

    * Conditional GAN (cGAN)
    * Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN)
    * Wasserstein GAN (WGAN)
    * StyleGAN
    * CycleGAN
  * Comprehended the motivations for these improved architectures.

### Key Learnings

* Gained a solid theoretical base on GANs before implementing them.
* Comprehended how adversarial learning works and helps generate realistic data.
* Comprehended how the Generator and Discriminator interact during the training process.
* Gained information on possible challenges and GAN architectures that resolve them.

## Week 2 – Implementation of Vanilla GAN

In the second week, I have implemented a **Vanilla GAN architecture** using **PyTorch** on **Anime Faces Dataset**. My main goal was to get hands-on experience of implementing a complete GAN training pipeline by developing both the Generator and Discriminator from scratch.

### Concepts Included

* **Anime Face Dataset**

  * Utilized the Anime Face Dataset from Kaggle.
  * Resized and normalized the images to a fixed size along with normalizing the pixel values between the range **[-1, 1]**.

* **Implementation of Generator**

  * Implemented a fully connected generator network with the help of Linear and ReLU layers.
  * Generated the anime face images by feeding random latent vectors as input.
  * Applied **Tanh** activation function in the output layer.

* **Implementation of Discriminator**

  * Developed a fully connected Discriminator network using Linear and LeakyReLU layers.
  * Classified the input images as **real** or **fake** images.
  * Made use of **Sigmoid** activation function in the output layer.

* **Project Structure**
    * `gan.py` – Implementation of the Vanilla GAN.
    * `generated_images/` – Directory storing the generated image samples after every epoch.
    * `checkpoints/` – Model weights of Generator and Discriminator saved during training.
    * `anime_dataset/` – Dataset containing the images used for training the model.

* **Implementation Details**
    * Automatic detection of GPU using CUDA when possible.
    * Preprocessing of custom dataset.
    * Setting fixed random seed to achieve reproducibility of results.
    * Saving generated image grids after each epoch to check the training process.
    * Saving checkpoints of trained models for further use.

### Key Learnings

* Acquired knowledge on the implementation of a Vanilla GAN.
* Learned the process of alternate training of the Generator and Discriminator in each iteration.
* Gained practical experience on dataset preprocessing, training of the model, and image generation using PyTorch.
* Witnessed the gradual improvement of generated images after each epoch as the Generator learns the distribution of the data.

## Week 3 – Datasets Preparation and DCGAN

The main topics during the third week were dataset preparation and Deep Convolutional Generative Adversarial Networks (DCGAN). During the current week, I understood the significance of dataset preparation for GAN training, and studied Deep Convolutional GANs, which use the convolutional neural network approach instead of using fully-connected layers to achieve better results.

### Week 3 Topics Covered

* **Dataset Preprocessing**

  * I have learned the necessity of data cleaning and preparation of image datasets prior to the training process.
  * Image resizing and normalization were studied.
  * The benefits of proper preprocessing for better training and improved model performance were understood.

* **Dataset Labeling**

  * The methods of labeled datasets creation for Conditional GANs were studied.
  * The methods of using **hair color**, **eye color**, and **hair length** as the labels for images were learned.
  * I understood how to organize the datasets using the `images/` folder along with `labels.csv`.

* **Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN)**

  * Learned how DCGAN surpasses the Vanilla GAN network design through replacement of fully connected layers with convolutional layers.
  * Learned how Strided Convolutions, Batch Normalization, ReLU, and LeakyReLU can be used for improving stability and image quality.
  * Learned why the use of convolutional layers is superior for image generation tasks.

* **Uses of DCGAN**

  * Image generation
  * Data augmentation
  * Learning features for image-related tasks
  * Generative modeling research

### Main Takeaways

* Learned about the importance of dataset preprocessing for the successful application of deep learning algorithms.
* Learned how datasets are preprocessed for conditional GANs through labeling based on simple image attributes.
* Learned the basics of DCGAN architecture and why convolutional layers allow much better results than vanilla GAN architecture.
* Learned how to create stable GAN models for high-quality image generation.

## Week 4 – Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs)

In the fourth week, I learned about **Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs)** which is the evolution of the Vanilla GAN, where one could generate images in a controlled way with the help of input labels. Unlike the Vanilla GAN, where random outputs were generated, the cGAN enables the generation of images having some desired features using the labels.

### Topics Covered

* **Introduction to Conditional GANs**

  * How Conditional GANs are the evolution of the Vanilla GAN?
  * How to condition the Generator and Discriminator with additional label information.
  * Differences between unconditional and conditional image generation.

* **Generator and Discriminator**

  * How does the Generator receive both a random noise vector and a condition (label)?
  * What does the Discriminator do when evaluating whether the image is real/fake along with checking the condition?

* **Controlled Image Generation**

  * How labels can be used for the generation of images having certain visual features.
  * The significance of building a well-labeled dataset for training the Conditional GANs.

* **Research Resources**

  * Reviewed the original Conditional GAN research paper by Mirza and Osindero.
  * Looked at the applications of DCGAN models as backbones for the creation of Conditional GANs.
  * Took guidance from the PyTorch tutorials for the implementation of cGANs.

### Key Takeaways

* Got to know how Conditional GANs give more control over the generation of results as compared to Vanilla GANs.
* Learned how labeling can be used to direct the Generator for generating images with certain traits.
* Learnt about the structure and working of Conditional GANs.
